🚌 Getting Around — Save Money on Transport

47|
48|
49|
1
50|
51| Compare Uber, Lyft & Taxis at the Airport 52|

Taxi from airport = flat rate $19–$27 by zone. Check Uber and Lyft too — they surge during conventions! Take whichever is cheapest. Taxis: have cash to avoid the credit card fee.

53|
54|
55|
56|
2
57|
58| Screenshot Your Airport Route 59|

Before your Uber/Lyft arrives, screenshot the GPS map from airport → hotel. If the driver goes a different way, you have proof. Prevents "long-hauling" scams!

61|

3
64|
65| Ping Your Uber Inside the Terminal 66|

Uber/Lyft drivers wait at UNLV and need drive time. Request while still inside so the car arrives as you reach the pickup area — saves waiting time!

72| Free Downtown Loop Shuttle 73|

Completely FREE shuttle runs through downtown Las Vegas all the way to the Strat. From there, connect to the Monorail for $5/ride along the Strip!

79| Deuce Bus — Unlimited Rides 80|

24-hour pass = $8 | 3-day pass = $20. Covers unlimited rides between downtown and the South Strip. Great for the whole team!

86| Free Trams — Use Them! 87|

Free tram: Bellagio → Park MGM. Another: Excalibur → Mandalay Bay. Get almost all the way across the Strip for free and save your feet!

93| Everything is FARTHER Than It Looks 94|

The Strip buildings are massive. What looks like a 5-min walk can be 45 minutes in 100°F desert heat. Use rides/trams for anything more than 1–2 hotels away.

100| Walk Through Hotels, Not the Street 101|

Walk through hotel interiors — air-conditioned, often faster, and you avoid the brutal sun. Example: walk through the Cosmopolitan to reach the Crystal shops (no stairs!)

🏨 Hotel & Money Tips

115|
116|
117|
10
118|
119| Know Your Resort Fees BEFORE Booking 120|

Hotels often show room price WITHOUT resort fees + taxes. The real cost can be much higher! Aria charges $55 resort fee — highest in Vegas. Always check the total price including all fees.

121|
122|
123|
124|
11
125|
126| Book Hotels Directly 127|

Book direct = you can call and get rate lowered if price drops! Third-party sites won't honor this. Always check the hotel website.

128|
129|
130|
131|
12
132|
133| The $20 Trick at Check-In 134|

Slip a folded $20 bill between your credit card and ID when handing over at the front desk. Politely ask "do you have any complimentary upgrades?" Works especially well at Planet Hollywood!

135|
136|
137|
138|
13
139|
140| Security Deposit Hold on Your Card 141|

Hotels put a $25–$200 hold on your card at check-in for "incidentals." Released at checkout. Know this so you don't accidentally overdraft your card!

142|
143|
144|

14
146|
147| SAHARA Guest Package = Great Value 148|

Look for the SAHARA package: auto-upgrade to Mara King room + $100 food & bev credit + coupon book with discounts on shows, Monorail, and more. Seen as low as $80/night all-in!

154| Mini Fridge Weighted Sensor Warning! 155|

Do NOT move anything in the hotel mini fridge unless you want to buy it — they're on weighted sensors and you'll automatically get charged. If you accidentally trigger it, go to front desk immediately.

161| Lock Your Door — Use the Safe 162|

You don't need a key card to access hotel floors. Fully lock your door every time. Use the in-room safe for laptop, jewelry, and gambling money. Non-negotiable!

168| Ask About Waiving Early Check-In Fees 169|

Early check-in at some hotels (like Aria) can cost $50–60. Politely ask at the front desk — if the hotel isn't fully booked, they'll sometimes waive it!

🍺 Food & Drink Tips

176|
177|
178|
18
179|
180| Stock Up at CVS or Walgreens 181|

Beer is $3.99 at CVS vs $16.99+ at casino bars. Same for water, snacks, sunscreen — everything is way cheaper. Load up the mini fridge on arrival!

182|
183|
184|
185|
19
186|
187| Free Casino Coffee in the Morning 188|

Skip the long Starbucks line! Head to the sports book or center bar — they brew fresh hot coffee that's cheaper and you won't wait. Morning hack!

189|
190|
191|
192|
20
193|
194| Watch for Hidden C&F Fees at Restaurants 195|

Some restaurants quietly add a ~4.9% "Convenience & Facility" fee in tiny print at the bottom of the menu. Ask for a paper receipt and have it removed! They'll usually say yes.

196|
197|
198|
199|
21
200|
201| Portions Are HUGE — Under-Order! 202|

It's hot, you're walking, you won't eat as much as you think. Order less than you think you want — you can always get a snack later. Split meals between 2 people to save money!

203|
204|
205|
206|
22
207|
208| The Strip Tax is Real 209|

The closer you are to the Strip, the more expensive EVERYTHING is. Even In-N-Out on the Strip costs more than other locations! Walk 5 minutes off-Strip or head downtown for real prices.

210|

214|
215| Split Drinks — Split Shots 216|

Drinks are pricey ($20+ for a Vegas Bomb shot). Ask for an extra cup and split it with a teammate. Drink responsibly and save money at the same time!

🎰 Gambling Tips

223|
224|
225|
24
226|
227| ALWAYS Check Slot Denomination First! 228|

Check what denomination the slot machine is BEFORE inserting money. A "10¢" machine × 800 lines = $80 per spin. Start with 1¢ machines to stay in control of your bets. Look before you play!

229|
230|
231|
232|
25
233|
234| Tipping Guide at the Casino 235|

Drinks on the casino floor: tip equal to what you're betting (minimum $2/tray). Sit-down restaurants: 20%. Bell services: tip when they bring bags up. Valet: tip when you get your car. Vegas is a tipping town!

240|
241| Avoid ATMs in Casinos 242|

Casino ATMs charge up to $11.99 per withdrawal! Use your bank's ATM or bring enough cash. Use the bill-breaking machines at casinos to get small bills for tips.

⚠️ Safety & Misc

249|
250|
251|
27
252|
253| Don't Jaywalk! 254|

Very common around the Paris Hotel area — but carries a BIG fine. Always use crosswalks. Cars move fast through Strip intersections!

255|

259|
260| Don't Touch the Revolving Doors 261|

Revolving doors at casino entrances are sensor-activated. Pushing them makes them STOP for safety. Keep your hands off — let the sensor do its job!

267| Dealing with Aggressive Street Sellers 268|

If someone is very pushy trying to give you "free" stuff on the street, just tell them you're a local. Works almost every time!

274| Free Entertainment Every Night! 275|

Fremont Street has 3 free concert stages with different acts every night — no cover, no tickets needed. Casino lounges all over the Strip also have free live music. Save money and still have a blast!
